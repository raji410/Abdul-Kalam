A.P.J Abdul Kalam

A.P.J Abdul Kalam was an Indian aerospace scientist and politician who served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007.

Here's a time line of A.P.J Abdul Kalam's life:

  • 1931-Born Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam on October 15, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu.
  • 1954-Abdul Kalam graduated with a degree in Physics.
  • 1960-Gains degree in Aeronautical Engineering from Madras Institute of Technology.
  • 1969-Kalam was transferred to ISRO as the project director of India's first satellite launch vehicle (SLV-III) to deploy the Rohini satellite in the earth's orbit.
  • 1980-1990-As the chief of the Integrated Guided Missile Development Programme, he was responsible for the development and operationalisation of AGNI and PRITHVI Missiles.
  • 1990-He has been honoured with the Padma Vibhushan.
  • 1992-1999-Works as Chief Scientific Advisor to DRDO.
  • 1997-He has been honoured with the Bharat Ratna.
  • 1999-2001-Principal Scientific Advisor to the Government of India.
  • 1999-He wrote and published Wings of Fire
  • 2002-07-Kalam was elected as the 11th president of India and has been called 'The People's President' many times in the media and by the masses.
  • 2015-He died on July 27.
“Don’t take rest after your first victory because if you fail in second, more lips are waiting to say that your first victory was just luck.”-A.P.J Abdul Kalam.
